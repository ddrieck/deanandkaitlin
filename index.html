Dean and Kaitlin

August 22nd, 2015

What She Said:

Dean first charmed me in keeper pants, flying through the air saving soccer balls. He asked me out and took me to pizza... I made him run a 5K and the rest is history. He never ran a 5K again.

A few fun facts about Dean that I think everyone ought to know. Dean is Dutch. His favorite color is orange, though he occasionally sports rave green in support of the Seattle Sounders. He is a self-diagnosed nerd and he'd be offended if you didn't agree. Oh, and Dean doesn't like red wine, chocolate, or beer. WHAT?!? I know. More for me!

More importantly, Dean shows me everyday what it means to really be yourself and own it. He reminds me not to take myself too seriously and to relax sometimes. Everyone needs a pajama day once in awhile. Dean loves me unconditionally and supports me wherever I go and whatever I do. I cannot wait to spend the rest of my life with him.

What He Said:

Did you know that Kaitlin dumped me three times when we first started going out? Oh she’ll deny it, saying things like “but we weren’t even going out” and “I can’t dump you if we aren’t dating”. "Semantics!" my wounded heart says.

Some of you might be surprised by these shaky beginnings, especially considering where we are now. The truth is, I believe that we both knew we belonged together. Maybe not in any overt way, but for some reason, despite each other’s foibles and rather hilarious missteps we always ended up together. And when we got together you couldn’t have asked for a more perfectly executed relationship. Yes it took 5 years to get engaged, but damn if we didn't plan this sucker out. From our Date Night bet to Kaitlin renting an empty apartment for 6 months, we were wonderfully methodical and us the entire way. In a way only we could be. Even though we said we never knew what was going to happen, I think in the back of our minds we knew this was for the good. The One.

I’ve had the joy of being with Kaitlin for 6 years, growing together, compromising together, pushing each other and making each other better people. I can’t wait for the next 60 (hopefully modern medicine can download my consciousness from my crippled old body at that time, but we can cross that bridge when we get there).

Places to Stay

Luxury

Willows Lodge is a luxury experience just steps from our wedding venue, with an award winning restaurant and proximity to Woodinville's local wineries.
http://www.willowslodge.com/

The Heathman Hotel is a beautiful boutique hotel on the water in downtown Kirkland with shopping and restaurants nearby.
https://www.heathmankirkland.com/

Seattle Tourist

The Mayflower Park Hotel is a boutique hotel in Seattle for those of you coming to Seattle for the first time. The Mayflower will bring you closer to shopping and Seattle sites including Pike Place Market, the Space Needle, the pier and a summer ferry ride. Tell the Mayflower you are with "PeopleFirm" and you will get a special hotel rate as well as discounted parking.
*Note: Seattle is a 30-40 minute drive from the wedding venue depending on traffic.
http://www.mayflowerpark.com/

Affordability

Hyatt House is a popular and more affordable option in nearby Redmond just 5 miles and a short taxi ride to the wedding venue.
http://redmond.house.hyatt.com/en/hotel/home.html

The Redmond Marriott Town Center is also a good option, especially for those not planning to rent a car. It is adjacent to restaurants, shopping, and movie theaters. Also, a short taxi ride to the wedding venue.
http://www.marriott.com/hotels/hotel-photos/seamc-redmond-marriott-town-center/
